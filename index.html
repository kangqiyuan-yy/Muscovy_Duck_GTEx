What is Duck GTEx?

As a multi-tissue regulatory genomics resource for Muscovy duck (Cairina moschata), the Muscovy Duck eQTL Atlas aims to build a public catalogue of genetic regulatory variants associated with gene expression across key metabolic and production-related tissues. This database integrates whole-genome sequencing data from 240 individuals, transcriptomic profiles from adipose, liver, and muscle tissues to support the systematic exploration of genotype-expression relationships in Muscovy ducks.

This resource provides a valuable reference for understanding tissue-dependent genetic regulation, rapid adaptation, lipid metabolism, muscle development, meat quality, and other economically important traits in waterfowl. By connecting genomic variation with transcriptional regulation and complex phenotypes, the Muscovy Duck eQTL Atlas offers a foundation for functional interpretation of trait-associated loci, molecular breeding, conservation genetics, and comparative avian genomics.

What's Inside

Whole-Genome Sequencing

High-coverage WGS data from 240 Muscovy duck individuals to capture comprehensive genetic variation across the genome.

Multi-Tissue Transcriptomics

RNA-seq profiles from adipose, liver, and muscle tissues — key tissues for lipid metabolism, energy homeostasis, and meat quality.

eQTL Mapping

Systematic identification of genetic variants regulating gene expression, with effect sizes, p-values, and distance-to-TSS annotations for 40,000+ records.

Functional Annotation

Connects regulatory variants to complex trait loci, supporting functional interpretation for molecular breeding and conservation genetics.

Population Genomics

Enables investigation of selection signatures and rapid adaptation in waterfowl through the lens of regulatory variation.

Comparative Avian Genomics

Serves as a reference for cross-species comparisons in avian regulatory genomics, bridging Muscovy duck biology with broader poultry science.
